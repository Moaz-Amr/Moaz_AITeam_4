The Gamma function extends the factorial to any real number, not just integers. Imagine factorial like 5! = 120; Gamma computes something like 3.5! ≈ 11.6.

It is defined by an integral from 0 to infinity, multiplying powers of x by an exponential decay curve to yield precise values.

In probability, it powers the Gamma distribution for modeling waiting times or positive quantities:

Example: A company predicts machine breakdown odds after 100 hours.

Example: Rainfall amounts on a rainy day for farming plans
